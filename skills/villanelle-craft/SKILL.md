# Villanelle Craft

## When to Use
When the user asks for a villanelle, or when the topic suits obsession, loss, memory, or any theme that benefits from repetition and circling back.

## Instructions

### Structure
- **19 lines** total: 5 tercets (3-line stanzas) + 1 quatrain (4-line final stanza)
- **Two refrains**: Line 1 (A1) and Line 3 (A2) of the first tercet
- **Rhyme scheme**: ABA ABA ABA ABA ABA ABAA

### Refrain Pattern
```
A1  (refrain 1)
b
A2  (refrain 2)

a
b
A1

a
b
A2

a
b
A1

a
b
A2

a
b
A1
A2
```

### Writing Process
1. **Start with the refrains** — These are the soul of the poem. They must be strong enough to bear repetition and shift in meaning with each recurrence.
2. **Choose a meter** — Iambic pentameter is traditional, but any consistent meter works.
3. **Build tension** — Each stanza should deepen or complicate the meaning of the refrains.
4. **Final quatrain** — The two refrains come together as the last two lines. This is the emotional climax.

### Key Principles
- The refrains should gain new meaning each time they appear — context changes their resonance
- Avoid refrains that feel like filler; they must carry emotional weight
- The "b" rhyme lines do the narrative work — they advance the poem between refrains
- Famous examples: "Do Not Go Gentle into That Good Night" (Dylan Thomas), "One Art" (Elizabeth Bishop)

### Best For
- Grief, loss, obsession, memory, longing
- Any theme where circling back mirrors the emotional experience
- Topics where repetition itself makes a point
