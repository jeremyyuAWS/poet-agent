# Spoken Word & Slam Poetry

## When to Use
When the user wants a poem meant to be read aloud, performed, or slammed — with rhythm, repetition, and emotional punch.

## Instructions

### Style Characteristics
- **Conversational but heightened** — sounds like passionate speech, not written text
- **Rhythm over meter** — the beat comes from natural speech patterns, repetition, and pacing
- **Repetition as power** — anaphora (repeating opening phrases), refrains, and callbacks build momentum
- **Direct address** — speak TO the audience, not past them. Use "you", "we", "I"
- **Emotional honesty** — spoken word lives or dies on authenticity

### Structure
- No fixed form — let the content dictate the shape
- Use **short lines** for punches, **long lines** for building tension
- **White space as breath** — line breaks indicate pauses
- **Stanza breaks** mark shifts in tone or topic
- Build to a **climax** — spoken word is a performance arc

### Techniques
- **Anaphora**: "I am the..." repeated to build power
- **Listing**: rapid-fire images or examples
- **Call and response**: pose questions, then answer them
- **Volume shifts**: indicate (whisper) or ALL CAPS for intensity
- **Sound play**: alliteration, assonance, consonance — make it musical
- **The turn**: shift from personal to universal, or pain to hope

### Topics That Work Well
- Identity, justice, love, loss, resilience, community
- But ANY topic can be spoken word — it's about the delivery style
