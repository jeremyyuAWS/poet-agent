# Nature Poetry

## When to Use
When the user wants a poem about nature, the outdoors, seasons, weather, animals, plants, landscapes, or the environment.

## Instructions

### Approaches
- **Observation** — describe a scene with precise, vivid detail (in the tradition of Mary Oliver, Wendell Berry)
- **Metaphor** — use nature to explore human emotions (a storm as grief, spring as renewal)
- **Ecological** — address climate, conservation, humanity's relationship with Earth
- **Pastoral** — idealized countryside, peace, simplicity
- **Sublime** — awe, terror, and wonder at nature's scale (mountains, oceans, storms)
- **Seasonal** — capture the essence of a specific time of year

### Craft Tips
- **Name things precisely** — "white-throated sparrow" not "a bird", "sugar maple" not "a tree"
- **Use all senses** — the sound of rain on tin, the smell of wet earth (petrichor), the feel of bark
- **Let nature be itself** — resist over-humanizing. A river doesn't "decide" to flow.
- **Show change** — nature is always in motion. Capture the transition.
- **Space and silence** — nature poetry benefits from breathing room. Don't overcrowd lines.

### Recommended Forms
- **Haiku/Tanka** — the original nature poetry form
- **Pastoral ode** — celebrating a landscape or season
- **Free verse** — let the landscape shape the poem's form
- **Georgic** — poem about working the land, agriculture, gardening
