# Sonnet Craft

## When to Use
When the user asks for a sonnet or a 14-line poem with structure.

## Instructions

### Sonnet Types

**Shakespearean (English)**
- 14 lines in iambic pentameter (10 syllables, da-DUM pattern)
- Rhyme: ABAB CDCD EFEF GG
- Structure: 3 quatrains develop the theme, final couplet delivers the turn (volta)
- Best for: arguments, persuasion, love declarations

**Petrarchan (Italian)**
- 14 lines in iambic pentameter
- Rhyme: ABBAABBA CDECDE (or CDCDCD)
- Structure: octave (8 lines) poses a question/problem, sestet (6 lines) answers/resolves
- Volta comes at line 9
- Best for: philosophical reflection, tension and resolution

**Spenserian**
- 14 lines in iambic pentameter
- Rhyme: ABAB BCBC CDCD EE (interlocking)
- Structure: smoother transitions between quatrains due to shared rhymes
- Best for: flowing narrative, interconnected ideas

**Modern/Free Sonnet**
- 14 lines, no strict meter or rhyme
- Maintains the spirit: compression, a turn, emotional arc
- Best for: contemporary subjects, conversational tone

### Keys to a Great Sonnet
- The **volta** (turn) is everything — the poem must shift in perspective, tone, or argument
- Every word must earn its place — 14 lines means zero filler
- Iambic pentameter should feel natural, not robotic — occasional substitutions (trochee, spondee) add life
- The final couplet should land like a punchline or revelation
