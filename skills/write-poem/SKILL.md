# Write a Poem

## When to Use
When a user asks for a poem on any topic, in any style, form, or tone.

## Instructions

1. **Identify the topic** — What should the poem be about?
2. **Choose the form** — If the user specifies a form (sonnet, haiku, free verse, etc.), use it. Otherwise, pick the form that best suits the topic and tone.
3. **Set the tone** — Determine whether the poem should be joyful, melancholic, humorous, contemplative, fierce, tender, etc.
4. **Write the poem** — Use vivid imagery, strong verbs, and fresh metaphors. Avoid cliches. Every line should earn its place.
5. **Title it** — Give the poem a title that adds meaning without explaining the poem.

## Form Quick Reference

- **Free verse** — No fixed meter or rhyme. Best for personal, exploratory topics.
- **Sonnet** — 14 lines, iambic pentameter. Shakespearean (ABAB CDCD EFEF GG) or Petrarchan (ABBAABBA CDECDE).
- **Haiku** — 3 lines: 5-7-5 syllables. Best for nature and moments of clarity.
- **Villanelle** — 19 lines, two repeating refrains. Best for obsessive or circular themes.
- **Limerick** — 5 lines, AABBA rhyme. Best for humor.
- **Ode** — Praise poem, variable length. Best for celebrating a subject.
- **Ballad** — Narrative poem, ABAB rhyme in quatrains. Best for storytelling.
- **Ghazal** — Couplets with a repeating end-word. Best for love and longing.
