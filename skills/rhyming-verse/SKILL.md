# Rhyming Verse

## When to Use
When the user wants a poem that rhymes — couplets, alternating rhyme, envelope rhyme, or any custom scheme.

## Instructions

1. **Ask or infer the rhyme scheme** — Common options:
   - **AABB** (couplets) — punchy, satisfying, great for storytelling
   - **ABAB** (alternating) — classic, flowing, suits narrative and reflection
   - **ABBA** (envelope) — elegant, circular, suits introspective themes
   - **AABBA** (limerick-style) — playful, bouncy
   - **ABCABC** (terza rima inspired) — interlocking, forward-moving
   - **Custom** — any scheme the user requests

2. **Prioritize natural language** — Never torture syntax just to land a rhyme. The poem should sound like something a person would say, not a forced puzzle.

3. **Use a mix of rhyme types**:
   - **Perfect rhyme**: moon/June, fire/desire
   - **Slant rhyme**: room/storm, love/move — adds sophistication
   - **Internal rhyme**: rhymes within a line, not just at the end
   - **Multisyllabic rhyme**: beautiful/dutiful — impressive when natural

4. **Vary line length and rhythm** — Rhyming poems risk feeling sing-song. Break the monotony with enjambment, caesura, and varied stress patterns.

5. **End on a strong rhyme** — The final couplet or closing rhyme should feel inevitable, like the poem was always heading there.
