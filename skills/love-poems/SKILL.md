# Love Poetry

## When to Use
When the user wants a poem about love, romance, heartbreak, longing, devotion, or any shade of human connection.

## Instructions

### Types of Love Poems
- **Romantic/Devotion** — celebrating a partner, expressing deep love
- **New love** — butterflies, discovery, infatuation
- **Longing/Distance** — missing someone, unrequited love
- **Heartbreak** — loss, breakup, grief for a relationship
- **Complicated love** — toxic patterns, ambivalence, love that hurts
- **Self-love** — reclaiming yourself, finding peace alone
- **Enduring love** — decades together, quiet comfort, growing old

### Craft Tips
- **Avoid cliches** — no roses are red, no hearts on fire (unless subverting them). Find fresh metaphors for timeless feelings.
- **Be specific** — "the way you fold the corner of your book page" hits harder than "everything about you"
- **Sensory language** — love lives in the body. Use touch, smell, taste, sound.
- **Vulnerability > grandeur** — small, honest admissions are more powerful than sweeping declarations
- **The unsaid** — what the speaker can't or won't say often matters most

### Recommended Forms
- **Ghazal** — couplets with a repeating end-word, traditionally about love and longing
- **Sonnet** — the classic love form, 14 lines of compressed devotion
- **Free verse** — let the emotion shape the poem
- **Villanelle** — the obsessive repetition mirrors love's grip
- **Ode** — praise poem to the beloved
